Innovative Pathways Program (IPP)

The Innovative Pathways Program (IPP) provides course offerings for students seeking course credit in support of individualized student goals centered around academic opportunity.

Program Description

Evening Program

The Evening Program offers full year classes that meet face-to-face 3 days a week, as well as single semester classes that meet once a week.

Dates

  • Full year: October 16 – May 16
  • Semester 1: October 16 – January 17
  • Semester 2: January 29 – May 16

Eligible Grade Levels

Current 9th – 12th grade students

Cost

  • Original Credit: $550
  • Credit Recovery: $50

Location

Homewood Center

Times

  • Period 1: 5:00-6:20 p.m.
  • Period 2: 6:25-7:00 p.m.

Meeting Days

  • Blended classes meet in person one day a week.
  • Face-to-Face classes meet in person three days a week.

Transportation

Two bus stops available at The Mall in Columbia and Harper’s Choice Village Center.

Summer Program

The Summer Program offers a range of courses during the mornings and afternoons over a 3 to 6 week period from June through August.

Dates

  • Full Credit Courses - June 24-August 2
  • Semester I Courses - June 24-July 12
  • Semester II Courses - July 15- August 2

Eligible Grade Levels

Rising 9th-12th grade students and current high school students.

Cost

  • Original Credit: $550
  • Single Semester: $325

Location

Hammond and Atholton High Schools

Times

  • Hammond High School: 7:30 a.m.-12:15 p.m.
  • Atholton High School, face-to-face: 8:00 a.m.-12:45 p.m.

Meeting Days

  • Face-to-Face classes meet five days a week in person

Registration

Registration for Summer School (High School) takes place in two ways: online or on-site at the Homewood Center.

Enrollment in course sections is limited. Current high school students will be placed in courses according to the order in which their requests are submitted. Priority will apply to students who need classes to graduate in August. Enrollment status will be communicated as soon as possible following each registration window.

Register

Online Registration

Online registration requests are submitted through the Synergy Parent Portal at the HCPSS Connect web page.

This year, there will be two online registration start periods for summer school courses:

  • February 1, beginning at 6 p.m.- February 15, ending at 6 p.m.: English, mathematics, science and social studies courses
  • March 1, beginning at 6 p.m.- March 15, ending at 6 p.m.: All courses listed above plus art, CRD, ESOL, Gear-Up for 9th Grade, Health, Lifetime Fitness and world language courses

All course registrations will continue to be available between the 1st and 15th of each month, from April through June, until course sections are filled and any student not placed in a course will be automatically added to applicable course wait lists. Multiple course requests may be submitted for varying courses occuring at the same time, or courses occurring in both the morning and afternoon.

On-Site Registration

On-site registration will be available at the Homewood Center on the following dates:

  • February 1; March 1 from 6:00–8 p.m.
  • February 6; March 20, April 3; May 15; and June 12; from 4:30 - 7 p.m.
  • June 15 and 22; from 9–11 a.m.

After the February and March registration periods close, and current high school students have been placed, lotteries will be held for rising 9th grade students to fill the available remaining seats. Once a course is at capacity, lottery results will be used as a wait list and all future registration requests will be added to the end of that list.

Payment

Payment should not be made until you have received confirmation that your counselor has approved your student’s course request.

An initial payment of 25% is due before the 30th of the month for students registering before May 15.

Accepted Payments

Online

Credit card payments (Visa or Mastercard) will be accepted online at: https://osp.osmsinc.com/HowardMD/. There will be a 4% processing fee for all online payments.

In Person

Bring your registration payment form to a scheduled on-site registration session with cash, check, money order, or credit card information.

In person payments are by appointment only. Please contact Alice Giles at 410-313-6627 to schedule an appointment.

Financial Assistance

Students currently receiving Free and Reduced Meals (FARMs) through HCPSS automatically qualify for 50% tuition reduction.

Any student may apply for 50% off full or reduced tuition by demonstrating financial hardship.

Applying

Send the financial assistance form with the required documentation to the Innovative Pathways Office.

  • Students enrolling in credit recovery courses are not eligible for tuition reduction due to the already reduced cost of the courses.
  • No financial assistance will be approved without documentation. Students in temporary housing situations may request a fee waiver from their home school's Pupil Personnel Worker.
  • The Financial Assistance Committee reviews all requests during registration and will contact eligible families.
