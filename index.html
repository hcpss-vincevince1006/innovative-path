Innovative Pathways Program (IPP)

The Innovative Pathways Program (IPP) provides course offerings for students seeking course credit in support of individualized student goals centered around academic opportunity.

Program Types

Evening Program

The Evening Program offers full year classes that meet 3 days a week, as well as single semester classes that meet once a week.

Dates

  • Full year: October 16 – May 16
  • Semester 1: October 16 – January 17
  • Semester 2: January 29 – May 16

Eligible Grade Levels

Current 9th – 12th grade students

Tuition

  • Original Credit: $550
  • Credit Recovery: $50

Times

  • Period 1: 5:00-6:20 p.m.
  • Period 2: 6:25-7:00 p.m.

Meeting Days

  • Blended classes meet in person one day a week.
  • Face-to-Face classes meet in person three days a week.

Transportation

Two bus stops available at The Mall in Columbia and Harper’s Choice Village Center.

Summer Program

The Summer Program offers a range of courses during the mornings and afternoons over a 3 to 6 week period from June through August.

Dates

June 24-August 2

Eligible Grade Levels

Current 8th-12th grade students.

Cost

Original Credit: $550

Location

Hammond and Atholton High Schools

Times

  • Hammond: 7:30 a.m.-12:15 p.m.
  • Atholton, in-person: 8:00 a.m.-12:45 p.m.
  • Atholton, blended: 1:30 p.m.-4:00 p.m.

Meeting Days

  • Blended classes meet three days a week in person
  • Face-to-Face classes meet five days a week in person

Transportation

Limited Transportation available for qualified students

Financial Aid Options

Students currently receiving Free and Reduced Meals (FARMs) through HCPSS automatically qualify for 50% tuition reduction. Any student may apply for 50% off full or reduced tuition by demonstrating financial hardship.

Applying

Send the “Financial Assistance Form” (found in Synergy under Parent View Portal) with the required documentation to the Innovative Pathways Office.

  • Students enrolling in credit recovery courses are not eligible for tuition reduction due to the already reduced cost of the courses.
  • No financial assistance will be approved without documentation. Students in temporary housing situations may request a fee waiver from their home school's Pupil Personnel Worker.
  • The Financial Assistance Committee reviews all requests during registration and will contact eligible families.
